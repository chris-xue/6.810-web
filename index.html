What is Flexibelt?

Belts are a piece of clothing almost everyone utilizes at one point or another - but sometimes having to manually adjust a belt can be annoying and embarassing in public situations. Whether it be after eating a large meal or changing into a different position, it can be a struggle to constantly have to adjust your belt to be more comfortable.

We designed Flexibelt to adaptively change its tightness a level for the user, without having to touch the belt. By using a force sensor situated near the buckle of the belt and two servo motors, we are able to continuously adjust the belt's tightness as the user changes position.

How It Works

Sensors

Flexibelt uses a force sensitive resitor (fsr) to sense the amount of force being applied to the belt by the body. We assocate a higher force with a tighter belt, and a lower force with a looser belt. The fsr is connected to an arduino which uses an algorithm to most cleanly tighten and loosen the belt.

Actuators

Flexibelt uses a pair of servo motors with attached customed printed gears to tighten and loosen the belt. These motors respond to the amount of force sensed by the force sensitive resistor and turn one direction or the other accordingly.

User Study

We ran user studies on three participants, who transitioned between standing and sitting/bloating positions using both a normal belt and our adaptive Flexibelt. Each particpant repeated this three times. Between each set of trials, we took note of any particpant feedback related to comfort and also took note of any feedback given during our post study questionnaire.

We found that all of our partipants could see the value in not having to adjust their belt, and did find the adaptive belt more comfortable when in a seated and bloated position. They also gave us some concerns about the noise level of the servos, and the belts jerky back and forth tightening/loosening due to not robust code. All in all, all our partipants would be interested in using a belt similar to Flexibelt if implemented more sleekly and responsive.

Implementation Challenges

Trouble with servo strength and grip of gears

Trouble positioning pressure sensor

Trouble writing code that ignores noise and tightens/loosens smoothly
